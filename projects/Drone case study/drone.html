Context

This project was done as a client engagement for Kerala Police, focused on designing a centralised platform to manage drone-based video surveillance, AI-assisted monitoring, and reporting.

Unlike consumer or startup products, this system was designed for live public safety operations, where decisions affect real people, real events, and real outcomes. The platform needed to support multiple roles — administrators, drone operators, analysts, and viewers — each with very different responsibilities, permissions, and cognitive demands.

Certain operational details and screens have been intentionally simplified or omitted in this case study to respect confidentiality and security requirements.

The Problem Space

The client brief described a system where drones capture large volumes of video across different locations and events — traffic monitoring, crowd control, perimeter surveillance, and incident response.

Some key realities shaped the problem:

  • Multiple teams interact with the same data, but should not have the same level of access
  • AI can assist detection, but human judgment must remain central
  • Alternative login with Google / Apple for faster access
  • Live operations require speed, while analysis and reporting require accuracy
  • The system must be usable in calm environments and high-pressure situations

Role Separation

The platform was intentionally divided into four primary roles: (Each role has its own mental model, risks, and priorities)

Designing for Responsibility

Instead of asking “What should this screen show?”, the guiding questions became:

  • What decisions is this person allowed to make?
  • What information do they need now, and what can wait?
  • What should they never be able to do?
  • How do we prevent misuse without slowing down real work?

This thinking led to a strict role-based structure, where the same system behaves very differently depending on who is signed in.

A User, whatever their role is, should never feel rushed but should never feel lost either.
Admin
Oversees system health, access, and compliance

According to the RFP, Admins are responsible for control, governance and orchestration of the overall system.

Admin Responsibilities:

  • Govern People → User & Role Management
  • Govern Assets → Drone Management
  • Govern Data Flow → Video Management
  • Govern Intelligence Output → Reports & Emergency Control

At its core, the Admin space revolves around these four responsibilities.

In surveillance systems, giving “power users” too much power isn’t ideal solution.

Design in Action

The real challenge was designing a clear, low-risk control layer that administrators could rely on, especially in time-sensitive situations.

# Admin Dashboard

Key System stats for quick operational health check and instant situational Awareness (not for deep analysis).

Real-time alerts positioned in a visually prominent area, as they need the highest priority - Reduces Risk situations.

Simplified activity visibility of streams (Read-only) , respecting role boundaries.

Admin should understand “what’s happening” in 5 seconds.

# Drone Fleet Management

Structured table layout designed for quick scanning and high information clarity.

Status chips (Online, Offline, Maintenance) for instant operational awareness.

Battery level & operator visibility support quick readiness checks and allocation decisions.

Search & filters positioned at the top for efficient record handling.

Inline actions to minimize unnecessary navigation and speed up routine tasks.

Disclaimer: The same design system and layout pattern is consistently applied across other modules to maintain predictability and reduce learning effort.

Design in Action

The Interface has to be minimal to reduce cognitive load during live operations and without distraction.

# Operator Dashboard

Assigned drones and Mission count blocks (active, pending, unresolved) surfaced at the top provide instant workload clarity overview in a glance.

Alert hierarchy separated by severity to reduce scanning time and cognitive effort during high-pressure situations.

Mission timeline adds temporal context without forcing navigation.

System health visibility of active drones (battery, signal, GPS) reduces mid-mission surprises.

# Live Mission Interface

Active Drone and lifecycle controls are highlighted to anchor attention on what’s currently in motion

Core flight metrics structured horizontally for quick scanning, reducing interpretation time during live operations.

Raw GPS and heading data prioritized, while the map remains secondary, supporting awareness without dominating decision space.

Clear separation of critical actions, destructive confirmations, and restrained color usage ensure speed without compromising operational safety.

Performance Metrics

If it takes more than a few seconds to assess readiness, the interface has failed already.

Design in Action

Analysts often review hours of drone footage to detect suspicious activity. The interface had to prioritize clarity, quick scanning, and efficient analysis without overwhelming the user.

# Analyst Dashboard

Workload summary cards (assigned, active, flagged, pending) appear at the top to give analysts an instant sense of what needs attention first.

Workload trend visualization helps understand task flow over time, revealing patterns in assignments, completions, and pending work.

Manual review vs AI detection chart increases transparency and builds trust in system intelligence/ AI assistance.

Event detection overview provides a quick snapshot of the types of incidents being detected across the system.

# AI Video Analysis Workspace

AI detection overlays highlight suspicious elements directly on the video feeds, for quick analysis.

Timeline scrubbing with detection markers helps analysts jump directly to moments where AI flagged activity instead of manually scanning the entire footage.

Incident marking and annotation tools allow analysts to document suspicious frames, add notes, and attach supporting evidence.

The right-side panel groups severity selection, tagging, and evidence uploads, keeping investigative actions separate from the video viewing area.

Once the review is complete, analysts can save the analysis for later continuation or generate the report to submit findings for administrative review.

The interface helps analysts move quickly from detection to documentation without losing context.

Performance Metrics

If analysts spend time searching for moments instead of analyzing them, the system is slowing intelligence work.

Design in Action

Viewer interfaces prioritize awareness, which aim to quickly understand ongoing situations without exposing complex operational controls.

# Viewer Dashboard

Status indicators at the top provide quick access to critical updates including live streams, reports, upcoming events, and recent alerts.

Drone location map helps viewers quickly understand geographic coverage and active monitoring zones.

Graphical summaries surface recent intelligence outputs without requiring deeper navigation.

# Live Stream Monitoring

Live stream cards present real-time drone footage with essential context such as location, drone ID, and timestamp.

AI detection badges highlight suspicious elements or detected objects directly on the feed preview.

Minimal interaction options ensure viewers can monitor events clearly without interfering with operational workflows.

Result: A command system that improves situational awareness while reducing decision friction.

System Strengths

Role-Based Architecture

Ensures each user only sees what they need while maintaining operational safety.

Operational Clarity

Critical alerts, missions, and detections surface immediately during time-sensitive situations.

Human + AI Collaboration

AI assists detection while human analysts retain control of final decisions.

Pressure-Ready Interface

Consistent layouts reduce learning effort and help teams act quickly.

Impact:
A structured surveillance platform that maintains full operational visibility while reducing decision friction during live operations.

Future Opportunities

  • Multiple analysts could review the same drone footage at the same time, adding notes and discussing observations together during critical incidents.
  • AI could also help prioritize alerts, highlighting the most critical detections so analysts can focus quickly during large-scale events.
  • A mobile companion interface would support field officers on the ground. Instead of relying only on the control room, they could quickly check verified intelligence reports and receive live updates directly from their phones.
  • Over time, Predictive insights from historical drone data could also help identify recurring risk zones or unusual activity patterns before they escalate.

Reflections

  • Designing for public safety systems requires thinking beyond interface aesthetics. Every design decision affects how quickly someone can understand and act.
  • To make complex operations feel clear and reliable for the people who depend on them, the design needs to guide attention so critical information can be grasped within the first few seconds.

In the end, the goal wasn’t just to design a usable interface, but to create a system that helps people stay aware, confident, and in control during critical situations.
