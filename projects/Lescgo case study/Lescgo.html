Travel Itinerary Planner

Lescgo

Designing a calm, end-to-end travel planning experience — from inspiration to navigation.

Overview

Planning a trip often feels more stressful than the trip itself. People jump between maps, social media, notes, and booking apps — saving places but never organizing them properly.

Lescgo brings discovery, planning, and navigation into one simple, guided flow.

The Problem

  • Too many apps for one journey
  • Saved places that never become a plan
  • Confusion once the trip starts
  • More planning than enjoying

Most travel apps show places well — but fail at structuring the journey.

My Role

UI/UX Designer

Scope

Problem framing

UI & interactions

Tool

Design Approach

Instead of designing features, I designed around travel phases:

Inspiration Decision Planning Execution

Each screen supports one clear mental task.

Map-Based Exploration

Maps help users judge distance, grouping, and feasibility instantly.

Bottom preview cards keep context — no full-screen interruptions.

Day-Wise Planning

Calendar-based planning with strong visual hierarchy.

Planning feels guided — not like filling a spreadsheet.

Outcome & Learnings

“Good UX doesn’t add more choices — it removes uncertainty.”

Lescgo focuses on clarity, reassurance, and momentum — helping users move confidently from “What should I do?” to “Let’s go.”
